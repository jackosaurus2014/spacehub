## 1. Executive Summary

LinkedIn is the single most important B2B marketing channel for SpaceNexus. The platform generates 80% of all B2B social media leads and delivers 277% more leads than Facebook and Twitter combined. For a space industry intelligence platform targeting enterprise buyers at $9.99-$29.99/month (with future enterprise tiers), LinkedIn is where our decision-makers live: VP Strategy, program managers, procurement directors, supply chain VPs, and space industry analysts.

**The core challenge**: LinkedIn's algorithm has shifted dramatically. Company pages now reach only 2-5% of followers organically -- a 60-66% decline since 2024. Personal profiles dominate 65% of content consumption. This means our strategy must be founder-led thought leadership first, company page second, and paid amplification third.

**Key recommendation**: Invest $3,500-$5,000/month total across Sales Navigator ($100/mo), LinkedIn Premium for the CEO ($60/mo), paid ads ($2,500-$4,000/mo), and a scheduling tool ($30/mo). Begin with 30 days of pure organic content to establish baseline engagement before activating paid campaigns.

### 3.5 A/B Testing Strategy: First 30 Days

**Week 1-2: Visual Testing (Ad Creative)**

Run 3-4 variations simultaneously, changing only the image/visual:
- Variant A: Dark space/satellite imagery with data overlay
- Variant B: Clean dashboard screenshot showing SpaceNexus platform
- Variant C: Market data chart/graph (e.g., launch cadence trends)
- Variant D: Person (CEO headshot or team working) -- humanized

Hold headline, body copy, CTA, and audience constant. Budget: $50/day split evenly across variants. Kill underperformers after 1,000 impressions each. Winner advances.

**Week 2-3: Headline Testing**

With winning visual, test 3 headline variations:
- Variant A: Pain point ("Tired of hunting for space market data across 20 sources?")
- Variant B: Social proof ("Join 500+ space professionals using SpaceNexus for market intelligence")
- Variant C: Curiosity/data ("The space economy just hit $570B. Here is what the data says is next.")

Same budget structure. Winner advances.

**Week 3-4: Audience Testing**

With winning creative + headline, test 3 audience segments:
- Segment A: Job title targeting (VP/Director at space companies)
- Segment B: Skills targeting (Satellite Communications + Market Intelligence)
- Segment C: Company list targeting (top 100 space companies employee list)

**Statistical significance**: Each test needs minimum 1,000 impressions per variant and 100+ clicks total to reach significance. At $50/day total, expect to complete one full A/B cycle in 14 days. Do not make decisions on less than 7 days of data.

## 4. Content Strategy Specifics

### 4.1 Post Formats Ranked by Engagement for B2B Data/Analytics

Based on 2026 LinkedIn algorithm data:

| Format | Avg Engagement Rate | Best Use for SpaceNexus | Frequency |
|--------|-------------------|------------------------|-----------|
| PDF/Document Carousel | 6.60% | Market data breakdowns, "5 trends in [topic]", product tours | 2x/week |
| Multi-Image Post | 5.85% | Before/after comparisons, data snapshots, module showcases | 1-2x/week |
| Native Video (under 90s) | 5.60% | CEO commentary on industry news, quick product demos | 1x/week |
| Text + Single Image | 2-5% | Thought leadership takes, industry reactions | 2-3x/week |
| Text-Only (long-form) | 0.5-2% | Hot takes, personal narratives, lessons learned | 1x/week |
| External Link Posts | Deprioritized by 60% | Avoid -- algorithm punishes external links | Rarely |

**Critical algorithm insight for 2026**: LinkedIn now deprioritizes external links by 60%. This means standard "check out our blog post [link]" content will get buried. Instead, deliver value natively in the post and put the link in the first comment. Or better yet, use Document Ads to drive link clicks through paid distribution.

**Dwell Time is the new king metric**: LinkedIn's algorithm in 2026 prioritizes how long people spend reading your content over how many likes it gets. Long-form carousels (8-12 slides), detailed text posts (1,200-1,500 characters), and native video all optimize for dwell time. Quick one-liner posts with a link will die in the algorithm.

**The Golden Hour**: The first 60 minutes after posting determine everything. LinkedIn shows your post to 2-5% of your network initially. Strong engagement in this window triggers second-degree (friends-of-connections) and third-degree amplification. If engagement is weak in the first hour, the post dies. This is why posting time and having an engagement pod (your team immediately engaging) matters enormously.

### 4.4 Building Authority: Articles vs. Posts vs. Carousels

**The Authority Stack (use all three in a hierarchy)**

| Format | Purpose | Frequency | Authority Value |
|--------|---------|-----------|----------------|
| **LinkedIn Articles** (1,500-3,000 words) | Deep-dive analysis; SEO-indexed by Google; establishes topical expertise | 1-2x/month | Highest long-term (evergreen, searchable) |
| **Carousel/Document Posts** (8-15 slides) | Data visualization, frameworks, step-by-step analysis | 2-3x/week | High (dwell time drives algorithmic reach) |
| **Text Posts** (500-1,500 characters) | Hot takes, industry reactions, personal narrative | 3-4x/week | Medium (builds voice and consistency) |
| **Comments on Others' Posts** | Demonstrate expertise where the audience already is | Daily (10-15 min) | High (borrowed audience; visible to prospects) |

**Article strategy**: Publish one LinkedIn article per month from the CEO profile. Topics should be "state of" pieces: "State of the Commercial Launch Market: Q1 2026 Data Analysis," "What the $3.5B SDA Award Means for the Supply Chain," "5 Data Points That Should Worry Every Satellite Operator." These articles rank in Google search results and serve as permanent authority content.

**Carousel strategy**: This is the workhorse format. Create data-driven carousels using SpaceNexus platform data:
- "2026 Launch Market: 7 Charts You Need to See" (carousel with actual SpaceNexus screenshots)
- "How the Space Economy Grew from $280B to $570B: A Visual Timeline"
- "The Complete Space Industry Regulatory Landscape in 12 Slides"

**Comment strategy**: This is the most underrated tactic. Spend 15 minutes daily commenting substantively on posts from Peter Beck, Mo Islam (Payload), Chris Quilty, and SpaceNews. Comments from a CEO with "Space Industry Intelligence Platform" in their headline get noticed. The comment must add value -- share a data point, a contrarian view, or a question that sparks discussion. Never comment "Great post!" -- that does nothing.

**Key InMail rules**:
- Keep messages under 100 words (22% higher reply rate)
- Always reference something specific about the recipient (recent post, company news, role change)
- Ask one clear question -- do not pitch features
- Send on Tuesday or Wednesday mornings
- Follow up once after 5-7 days, then stop. Never send more than 2 InMails to the same person

### 5.4 Connection Request Strategy

**Acceptance rate benchmark**: Personalized connection requests get 9.36% reply rate vs 5.44% without a message. Including a note increases acceptance by 58% in B2B tech.

**Connection Request Templates**

**After they engage with your content:**

> Hi [Name] -- noticed you commented on my post about [topic]. Thanks for the thoughtful response. Would love to connect and continue the conversation.

**After a shared event or webinar:**

> Hi [Name] -- we were both at [Space Symposium / SATELLITE Conference / webinar name]. Your perspective on [topic] was interesting. Let us connect.

**Cold but relevant:**

> Hi [Name] -- I see you lead [function] at [Company]. I run SpaceNexus, a space industry intelligence platform. I share a lot of market data and analysis here that might be useful for your work. Happy to connect.

**Volume targets**:
- Send 15-20 personalized connection requests per day (LinkedIn limit is ~100/week; stay under 80 to avoid restrictions)
- Expect 20-35% acceptance rate with personalized notes
- Target: 300-500 new high-quality connections per month
- Within 6 months: 1,500-3,000 targeted connections in the CEO's network

### Employee Advocacy Program (Even as a Small Team)

**Why this matters**: Employee-shared posts generate 561% more reach and 8x more engagement than company page posts. Even a 3-person team can multiply LinkedIn presence significantly.

**Implementation**:

1. **Weekly advocacy email (every Monday, 9 AM)**:
   - Provide 2-3 pre-written posts that team members can share as-is or customize
   - Include the "why" -- what the post is about and why it matters
   - Make sharing effortless: provide exact copy + image ready to paste

2. **Content types for team members**:
   - Engineers: "How we built [feature]" technical posts
   - Sales/BD: Industry insights they found while researching prospects
   - CEO: Thought leadership (already covered above)

3. **Rules of engagement**:
   - No one is forced to post -- advocacy must feel authentic
   - Team members should add their own voice, not copy-paste robotically
   - Engage with each other's posts within the first 30 minutes (Golden Hour amplification)

4. **Incentive**: Track who drives the most profile visits and leads. Recognize top advocates monthly. Even simple recognition ("Sarah's post this week drove 2,000 impressions") builds momentum.

5. **Scale trigger**: When 3+ team members are posting consistently (6+ weeks), invest in an advocacy platform like GaggleAMP or DSMN8 ($200-$500/month) to systematize content distribution.
