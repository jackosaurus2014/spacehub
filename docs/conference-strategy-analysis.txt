NETWORKING EVENTS
-----------------
This is where Space Symposium earns its Tier 1 status. The networking is
structured, premium, and explicitly designed for deal-making:

  Official Events:
  1. Opening Reception -- Large-scale networking event (included with registration)
  2. Government Affairs Breakfast -- Policy-focused networking with government,
     military, and industry leaders
  3. Space Symposium Dinners -- Formal seated dinners honoring military/space
     leaders (a la carte, $300-$600/ticket). These are black-tie-optional events
     where enterprise relationships are built. Sitting next to a program manager
     from Space Force or a VP at Lockheed is how contracts start.
  4. Track-specific receptions -- E.g., Space Law and Regulation reception
     hosted by Greenberg Traurig, AI & Space track events
  5. Exhibit Center reception hours -- Open networking in exhibit halls

  Unofficial/Off-Site Events:
  6. Company-hosted receptions -- Lockheed, Northrop, Boeing, and other primes
     host private dinners and receptions at Broadmoor restaurants and nearby
     venues. These are by invitation only.
  7. Broadmoor bar/lobby networking -- The Broadmoor lobby bar is legendary
     in aerospace circles. After formal events end, deal-makers congregate here.
     This is where the real conversations happen: 9 PM to midnight, hotel bar.
  8. Golf outings -- The Broadmoor has championship courses. Tee times during
     symposium week are prime networking opportunities.
  9. Startup/VC meetups -- Informal gatherings organized by VCs and accelerators

  Key Insight: The Broadmoor's all-in-one campus design (hotel + conference +
  restaurants + bars + golf in one location) creates a pressure cooker effect.
  You cannot leave without running into people. This is by design. This is
  how enterprise deals happen in aerospace -- repeated casual contact over 3-4
  days builds relationships that lead to contracts 6-12 months later.

HOW SMALL COMPANIES / STARTUPS GET MAXIMUM VALUE
-------------------------------------------------
Based on extensive research into startup strategies at Space Symposium:

  1. DO NOT get a booth your first year.
     A 10x10 booth costs $8,000-$12,000+ before construction, graphics, and
     staffing. As a pre-revenue startup, this is a poor investment. Instead:
     - Attend as a badge-holder
     - Book every available 1:1 meeting
     - Work the exhibit floor approaching relevant companies
     - Attend every open networking event
     - Be in the Broadmoor bar every evening

  2. Pre-schedule 15-20 meetings.
     Use LinkedIn and email to set up meetings with target prospects BEFORE
     the event. The Space Foundation app may offer meeting scheduling features.
     Start reaching out 6-8 weeks before (i.e., now -- we have 9 weeks).

  3. Host a small off-site reception.
     Instead of a booth, spend $1,500-$3,000 on a private dinner or cocktail
     reception at a Broadmoor restaurant or nearby venue for 15-20 invited
     prospects. This creates exclusivity and dedicated attention that a booth
     cannot match. Cost per meaningful conversation: $150-$200 (vs $400-$600
     per conversation at a booth with random foot traffic).

  4. Apply for speaking opportunities.
     The Innovate Pitch Competition is the primary startup slot. Additionally,
     the Space Generation Fusion Forum (April 11-13) offers lightning talks
     and panel participation with lower barriers to entry.

  5. Use the New Generation Space Leaders program.
     For professionals 35 and under, this program provides structured access
     to senior space leaders through mentorship and networking opportunities.
     Requires Standard Registration.

  6. Content strategy during the event.
     Live-tweet (X/LinkedIn post) key sessions, publish real-time analysis on
     SpaceNexus, and create "Day 1/2/3 Recap" content. This establishes thought
     leadership and attracts followers who could not attend.

BOOTH vs ATTENDANCE-ONLY ROI ANALYSIS
--------------------------------------
For a pre-revenue startup like SpaceNexus, the math on exhibiting is clear:

  ATTENDANCE ONLY (SATELLITE):
  - Cost: $0-$1,800 registration + $800-$1,200 travel = $800-$3,000 total
  - Expected quality conversations: 15-25 (from pre-scheduled meetings +
    walking the floor)
  - Cost per quality lead: $32-$200
  - Advantage: Flexibility, can attend sessions, lower risk

  EXHIBITING (SATELLITE):
  - Cost: $6,200 (booth) + $2,000-$5,000 (construction/graphics) +
    $800-$1,200 (travel) + $500 (materials) = $9,500-$12,900
  - Expected quality conversations: 30-50 (booth visitors + meetings)
  - Cost per quality lead: $190-$430
  - Disadvantage: Booth requires 2+ people to staff (one person cannot leave),
    many booth conversations are low-quality (tire kickers, students), cannot
    attend sessions while staffing booth

  HOSTING A RECEPTION INSTEAD (either event):
  - Cost: $1,500-$3,000 (venue, food, drinks for 15-25 invited attendees)
  - Expected quality conversations: 12-20 (all pre-qualified invitees)
  - Cost per quality lead: $75-$250
  - Advantage: Every attendee is a targeted prospect, creates exclusivity,
    deeper conversations than booth drive-bys

  VERDICT: For SpaceNexus's current stage, attendance-only or hosted reception
  delivers better ROI per dollar than exhibiting. Save booth investment for
  Year 2 when you have product-market fit validated and revenue to justify it.
