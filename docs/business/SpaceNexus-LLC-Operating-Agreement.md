**1.5 Term.** The Company shall have perpetual existence unless dissolved in accordance with Article X of this Agreement or the Act.

**1.6 Business Purpose.** The Company is organized for the following purposes:

(a) To develop, operate, maintain, and monetize a technology platform known as "SpaceNexus" (located at spacenexus.us and related domains), which provides space industry intelligence, data analytics, market research, news aggregation, satellite tracking, regulatory compliance tools, and related information services through web applications, mobile applications, and application programming interfaces (APIs);

## ARTICLE IV — PROFITS, LOSSES, AND DISTRIBUTIONS

**4.1 Allocation of Profits and Losses.** All profits and losses of the Company shall be allocated solely to the Member for each fiscal year.

**4.2 Distributions.** The Member shall be entitled to receive distributions from the Company at such times and in such amounts as the Member determines, subject to the following:

(a) The Company shall maintain adequate reserves for its operating expenses, debt obligations, capital expenditures, and any reasonably anticipated business needs;

(b) The Company shall not make any distribution that would render the Company unable to pay its debts as they become due in the ordinary course of business; and

(c) Distributions shall be made in compliance with Section 17-29-405 of the Act.

**4.3 Tax Distributions.** Notwithstanding Section 4.2, the Company shall distribute to the Member, prior to the due date (including extensions) for each taxable year, an amount sufficient to enable the Member to satisfy his or her federal and state income tax obligations attributable to the Company's taxable income allocated to the Member for such taxable year (the "Tax Distribution"). The Tax Distribution shall be calculated using the highest combined marginal federal and applicable state income tax rate in effect for the taxable year.

**5.1 Authority of Member.** The Member shall have the full and exclusive right to manage the Company's business and affairs. Without limiting the generality of the foregoing, the Member shall have the authority to:

(a) Enter into, execute, amend, supplement, acknowledge, and deliver any and all contracts, agreements, and other instruments in the name of and on behalf of the Company;

(b) Open, maintain, and close bank accounts and other financial accounts, and draw checks and other orders for the payment of money on behalf of the Company;

(c) Borrow money on behalf of the Company and, in connection therewith, execute and deliver promissory notes, bonds, debentures, deeds of trust, mortgages, pledges, security agreements, and other evidences of indebtedness;

(d) Purchase, lease, or otherwise acquire real and personal property;

(e) Sell, transfer, convey, assign, exchange, or otherwise dispose of Company property;

(f) Hire, compensate, and terminate employees, contractors, consultants, and agents;

(g) Engage attorneys, accountants, auditors, and other professionals;

(h) Pay all reasonable and necessary expenses of the Company;

(i) Make tax elections and file tax returns on behalf of the Company;

(j) Obtain insurance coverage for the Company and its assets;

(k) Enter into licensing agreements, service agreements, and technology vendor contracts necessary for the operation of the SpaceNexus platform, including but not limited to hosting services, payment processing services, email delivery services, data providers, and API service agreements;

(l) Establish, modify, and administer subscription pricing, billing terms, free trial periods, promotional offers, and refund policies for the Company's SaaS products;

(m) Register, maintain, renew, and protect the Company's intellectual property, including domain names, trademarks, copyrights, and trade secrets;

(n) Enter into data processing agreements, privacy frameworks, and compliance certifications as required by applicable law or business needs; and

(o) Take any and all other actions necessary, appropriate, or advisable in furtherance of the Company's business purposes.

**5.2 Third-Party Reliance.** Any person or entity dealing with the Company may rely upon the authority of the Member to bind the Company without inquiry into the provisions of this Agreement or compliance herewith.

**5.3 Compensation.** The Member shall be entitled to receive reasonable compensation for services rendered to the Company, as determined by the Member. Such compensation shall be treated as a guaranteed payment under Section 707(c) of the Internal Revenue Code and shall be deducted by the Company as a business expense.

**5.4 Reimbursement of Expenses.** The Company shall reimburse the Member for all reasonable and documented out-of-pocket expenses incurred by the Member in connection with the management and operation of the Company's business, including but not limited to:

(a) Hosting and infrastructure costs (e.g., Railway, cloud services, CDN);

(b) Third-party software and API subscriptions (e.g., payment processing, email services, data providers);

(c) Domain name registration and renewal fees;

(d) Professional services (legal, accounting, consulting);

(e) Marketing and advertising expenses;

(f) Travel expenses incurred for Company business; and

(g) Office supplies, equipment, and other business necessities.

**5.5 Officers and Agents.** The Member may appoint officers, agents, or representatives to act on behalf of the Company, and may delegate authority to such persons as the Member deems appropriate. Any such appointment or delegation may be revoked by the Member at any time.

## ARTICLE VI — TECHNOLOGY AND DIGITAL ASSETS

**6.1 Platform Assets.** The following digital and technology assets are the sole property of the Company and shall be treated as Company assets for all purposes under this Agreement:

(a) **Domain Names:** All domain names registered in connection with the Company's business, including spacenexus.us and related domains;

(b) **Source Code and Software:** All proprietary source code, compiled code, scripts, configuration files, and software comprising the SpaceNexus platform, whether stored in version control repositories (e.g., GitHub), cloud services, or local environments;

(c) **Databases and Data:** All databases, data stores, user data, subscriber information, company profiles, news archives, and other structured or unstructured data collected, created, or maintained by the Company;

(d) **Third-Party Accounts:** All accounts with third-party service providers used in the operation of the business, including but not limited to hosting providers (Railway), payment processors (Stripe), email services (Resend), version control (GitHub), analytics services, and API data providers;

(e) **API Keys and Credentials:** All API keys, secret keys, tokens, certificates, and other credentials used to access third-party services or authenticate the Company's own API products;

(f) **Brand Assets:** All logos, design files, icons, illustrations, and visual assets associated with the SpaceNexus brand; and

(g) **Customer Data:** All subscriber accounts, email lists, newsletter subscribers, payment records, and customer relationship data, subject to applicable privacy laws and the Company's Privacy Policy.

**6.2 Data Protection Obligations.** The Company shall:

(a) Maintain reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal data collected through the SpaceNexus platform;

(b) Comply with applicable data protection laws, including but not limited to the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) to the extent applicable, and CAN-SPAM Act requirements;

(c) Maintain a current Privacy Policy and Terms of Service accessible on the SpaceNexus website;

(d) Implement and maintain appropriate access controls, encryption, and security practices for user data and payment information; and

(e) Promptly address any data breach or security incident in accordance with applicable notification laws.

**6.3 Open-Source Compliance.** To the extent the Company's software incorporates open-source components, the Member shall ensure compliance with applicable open-source licenses and shall maintain a record of open-source dependencies and their respective license terms.

**6.4 Backup and Disaster Recovery.** The Member shall ensure that reasonable backup and disaster recovery procedures are maintained for the Company's critical digital assets, including source code repositories, databases, and configuration data.

## ARTICLE VII — TAXES AND ACCOUNTING

**7.1 Tax Treatment.** For federal income tax purposes, the Company shall be treated as a disregarded entity pursuant to Treasury Regulation Section 301.7701-3, unless the Member elects otherwise. All items of income, gain, loss, deduction, and credit of the Company shall be reported on the Member's individual federal and state income tax returns.

**7.2 Tax Elections.** The Member may make any tax election available to the Company under the Internal Revenue Code or applicable state tax laws, including but not limited to:

(a) An election to be taxed as an S corporation under Section 1362 of the Internal Revenue Code;

(b) Elections relating to the method of accounting; and

(c) Any other elections deemed appropriate by the Member or the Company's tax advisor.

**7.3 Fiscal Year.** The fiscal year of the Company shall be the calendar year (January 1 through December 31), unless the Member elects a different fiscal year.

**7.4 Books and Records.** The Member shall maintain, or cause to be maintained, complete and accurate books and records of the Company's business and financial affairs, including:

(a) A current list of the full name and last known address of the Member;

(b) A copy of the Articles of Organization, this Operating Agreement, and all amendments thereto;

(c) Copies of the Company's federal and state income tax returns for the three (3) most recent fiscal years;

(d) Copies of the Company's financial statements for the three (3) most recent fiscal years;

(e) Records of all capital contributions and distributions;

(f) Records of subscription revenue, API access revenue, advertising revenue, and other income streams; and

(g) Records of all material contracts and agreements to which the Company is a party.

**7.5 Accounting Method.** The Company's books shall be kept using the cash method of accounting, or such other method as the Member or the Company's accountant determines to be most appropriate, consistently applied.

**7.6 Bank Accounts.** The Company shall maintain one or more bank accounts in the name of the Company at a financial institution selected by the Member. All Company funds shall be deposited in such accounts. The Member shall not commingle Company funds with personal funds.

## ARTICLE VIII — LIABILITY AND INDEMNIFICATION

**8.1 Limited Liability.** The Member shall not be personally liable for any debt, obligation, or liability of the Company, whether arising in contract, tort, or otherwise, solely by reason of being a Member, manager, or agent of the Company. This limitation of liability applies to the fullest extent permitted by the Act.

**8.2 Indemnification of Member.** The Company shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Member and the Member's heirs, executors, administrators, legal representatives, successors, and assigns (each, an "Indemnified Person") from and against any and all claims, demands, losses, damages, liabilities, costs, and expenses (including reasonable attorneys' fees and court costs) arising out of or relating to any act or omission of the Member in connection with the Company's business, provided that the Member acted in good faith and in a manner the Member reasonably believed to be in, or not opposed to, the best interests of the Company, and provided further that the Member's conduct did not constitute fraud, willful misconduct, or a knowing violation of law.

**8.3 Indemnification of Agents.** The Company may, at the discretion of the Member, indemnify employees, officers, agents, and contractors of the Company to the same extent as provided in Section 8.2 with respect to the Member.

**8.4 Insurance.** The Member may cause the Company to obtain and maintain insurance, at the Company's expense, to protect the Company, the Member, and any officers, employees, or agents of the Company against any liability, loss, or expense arising out of the Company's business, including but not limited to:

(a) General commercial liability insurance;

(b) Professional liability / errors and omissions insurance;

(c) Cyber liability and data breach insurance;

(d) Directors and officers (D&O) insurance (if applicable); and

(e) Such other insurance as the Member deems appropriate.

**8.5 Exculpation.** No Indemnified Person shall be liable to the Company for any act or omission performed or omitted by such Indemnified Person in good faith on behalf of the Company and in a manner reasonably believed by such Indemnified Person to be within the scope of authority conferred on such Indemnified Person by this Agreement, except for acts or omissions constituting fraud, willful misconduct, or a knowing violation of law.

## ARTICLE IX — TRANSFER OF MEMBERSHIP INTEREST

**9.1 Transfer.** The Member may freely sell, assign, transfer, convey, pledge, encumber, or otherwise dispose of all or any portion of the Member's membership interest in the Company at any time, subject to applicable law.

**9.2 Transfer of Digital Assets.** In connection with any transfer of membership interest, the transferring Member shall ensure the orderly transfer of all Company digital assets to the transferee or the Company, including but not limited to:

(a) Access credentials and administrative accounts for all third-party services;

(b) Source code repository access (GitHub or equivalent);

(e) Hosting and infrastructure account access (Railway);

(f) Database access credentials; and

**9.3 Assignee's Rights.** Unless an assignee of a membership interest is admitted as a member of the Company, such assignee shall have no right to participate in the management of the Company's business and affairs and shall only be entitled to receive the allocations and distributions attributable to the assigned membership interest.

## ARTICLE X — DISSOLUTION AND WINDING UP

**10.1 Events of Dissolution.** The Company shall be dissolved upon the first to occur of the following:

(a) The written election of the Member to dissolve the Company;

(b) The entry of a decree of judicial dissolution pursuant to the Act;

(c) The death, incapacity, or bankruptcy of the Member, unless a successor is designated pursuant to Article XI of this Agreement; or

(d) Any other event that causes the dissolution of the Company under the Act.

**10.2 Winding Up.** Upon dissolution, the Company's affairs shall be wound up as follows:

(a) The Member (or the Member's legal representative or successor) shall act as the liquidating agent;

(b) The liquidating agent shall collect and sell or otherwise liquidate the Company's assets, including digital assets, in a commercially reasonable manner;

(c) The proceeds of liquidation shall be applied in the following order of priority:

> (i) Payment of the Company's debts and liabilities to creditors, including any amounts owed to third-party service providers, in the order of priority as provided by law;
>
> (ii) Setting up of any reserves that the liquidating agent deems reasonably necessary for contingent or unforeseen liabilities or obligations of the Company;
>
> (iii) Payment of any remaining balance to the Member.

(d) The liquidating agent shall ensure that all user data is handled in accordance with the Company's Privacy Policy and applicable data protection laws during the winding-up process, including providing users with reasonable notice and the opportunity to export their data;

(e) Upon completion of winding up, the liquidating agent shall file Articles of Dissolution with the Wyoming Secretary of State.

**10.3 No Obligation to Restore Deficit.** In no event shall the Member be obligated to restore a deficit balance in the Member's capital account.

(c) **Interim Management.** During any transition period, the Member's legal representative (executor, administrator, or guardian) shall have the authority to manage the Company's business and affairs to preserve the value of the Company's assets, including maintaining the operation of the SpaceNexus platform, renewing critical service agreements, and fulfilling existing customer obligations.

This record shall be updated no less frequently than annually.

**14.4 Headings.** The article and section headings in this Agreement are for convenience of reference only and shall not affect the interpretation of this Agreement.

**14.5 Binding Effect.** This Agreement shall be binding upon and inure to the benefit of the Member and the Member's heirs, executors, administrators, successors, and assigns.

**14.6 No Third-Party Beneficiaries.** This Agreement is for the sole benefit of the Member and the Company. Nothing in this Agreement shall confer any rights or remedies upon any person or entity other than the Member and the Company.
