**This Thursday, NASA rolls the most powerful rocket ever built to the launchpad. Here's why it matters for the entire space industry.**

Artemis II is heading to Pad 39B at Kennedy Space Center on March 20, carrying the first crew to fly beyond Earth orbit in over 50 years.

Quick facts about Artemis II:

- 4 astronauts: Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, Christina Koch, and Jeremy Hansen (CSA)
- 10-day mission around the Moon and back — the first crewed lunar flyby since Apollo 17 in 1972
- Powered by the Space Launch System (SLS), producing 8.8 million pounds of thrust at liftoff
- Orion spacecraft will travel ~400,000 km from Earth, farther than any crewed vehicle in history
- First Canadian astronaut to fly to the Moon

This isn't just a NASA mission. Artemis II validates the architecture for the entire Artemis program — including Artemis III's crewed lunar landing using SpaceX's Starship HLS, the Gateway lunar space station, and a growing ecosystem of commercial lunar services (CLPS, spacesuits, rovers, habitats).

The downstream impact reaches across the space industry: launch providers, satellite operators, ground systems, life support, and space tourism are all building on this foundation.
